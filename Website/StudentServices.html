Student support services descriptions and links

Talk to us university portal

Your MyHull university portal is an important tool when accessing support or directing queries to faculties and personal supervisors. The talk to us page within your MyHull portal can give you the chance to do the following, but not limited to:

  • Contacting your faculty to apply for extensions and mitigating circumstances
  • Contacting your personal supervisor for a meeting or general queries
  • Apply for other support services, whether that be for financial, mental, or disability support
To access your talk to us page, please click on either of the two following links:
  • My Hull Portal
  • Mental health web page on the university website

Disabled students allowance (DSA)

DSA is a grant available to UK students with a health condition, disability, or a learning disability, which can help by paying any extra study related costs you might incur at university. Support can include:

  • Non-medical helpers and study skills for dyslexia and can provide note-taking support for sensory impaired students
  • Specialist study equipment such as: computers and assistive software
  • Disability related travel support around the university
The DSA is a government scheme, but the university will help you fill out the relevant forms. There is no timeframe on a DSA application, and you will need a copy of medical evidence for your health condition, disability, or learning disability. To check if you’re eligible or for extra information on the DSA grant, please follow the links below:
  • Disabled students allowance webpage on the university website
  • DSA webpage on the government website

Student assistance programme (SAP)

SAP is a 24-hour helpline which assists anything impacting your wellbeing and mental health. This service is partnered with Health Assured and is free to call anytime during the day, where you will talk with a counsellor. You might contact the service if you’re feeling:

  • Stressed
  • Lonely
  • Homesick
  • Anxious
  • Dealing with bereavement
SAP is open to all students enrolled with the University, regardless of age, nationality, and full-time or part-time students. The number for this service is: 0800 028 3766. For any more information on SAP or Health Assured, please follow the two links below:
  • Student assistance programme webpage on the university website
  • Health Assured website

Specific learning differences (SpLD)

SpLD is a service which support students with learning difficulties like; dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia, and ADHD. The university on applying to SpLD will send you a questionnaire to establish how they can support you. The support the university can provide is:

  • DSA funded specialist study support
  • A designated virtual learning site with various resources to aid your studies
  • Dedicated tutors that work closely with your department to promote an inclusive learning environment
  • Assistive software which helps you read and write
  • Online screeners to identify dyslexia, dyspraxia, visual difficulties, attention, and concentration issues
  • Advice and support on application for the DSA grant
  • Reasonable adjustments (extra time in exams, deadline extensions and mitigating circumstances)
To apply you will need to provide evidence to the university, this could be any of the following:
  • Psychologist report
  • Therapy report
  • Initial screening report
  • Clinical report
For more information on the SpLD service, please click on the following link which is a link to the SpLD page on the university website:
  • https://www.hull.ac.uk/choose-hull/student-life/student-support/specific-learning-differences

Lets Talk

Talk to us is an external service that the university refers students with issues pertaining towards their mental health and wellbeing. They offer treatment for an array of issues such as:

  • Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT)
  • Bereavement support
  • Depression counselling
  • Depression counselling for couples
  • Eye movement desensitisation and reprocessing (EMDR)
  • Interpersonal therapy (IPT)
  • Guided self-help
To access and apply for this support service, contact the university to set up a meeting about the issues you’re experiencing. The university will then refer you to Let’s talk, or you can click on the link which will take you to the Let’s talk website:
  • https://www.letstalkhull.co.uk/

Hardship support fund

This is fund for UK/ home students that receive a maintenance funding but are experiencing unforeseen financial difficulties. To enrol you must have accessed all financial support available to you previously and must have received the first instalment of your student finance payment. Evidence must be provided and if you’re eligible, the hardship fund can offer you the following support:

  • The summer fund: This provides support during the summer period and is for non-final year students who are with depends, unable to work due to illness or disability, and students who are eligible for the Humber Grant
  • Short term solutions: Provides food vouchers, bus card and other alternative essentials when a student is not receiving student loans on time. The student will have to provide evidence of a student funding application and evidence to support the request for your student loan
  • Discretionary funds: A limited fund available to students experiencing unforeseen financial circumstances like homelessness, relationship breakdowns, and family bereavements
For more information on the Hardship fund and the separate support the fund provides, please follow the links below:
  • Financial support webpage on the university website

Hull sport participation fund

This grant helps students to participate in the university’s extracurricular activities such as: sports and societies. The difference with this grant is that thew application process is competitive and not all applications will be successful. To be eligible you must meet one of the following criteria:

  • A student with a household income of £25,000 or less
  • An international or EU student
  • A mature student
  • A student who has declared a disability
  • A student assessed as estranged by Student Finances England
  • A student with dependents
  • A student who is a carer
For more information on the Hull sport participation funds and applications to apply for this fund, please follow the links below:
  • Financial support webpage on the university website
  • Participation fund individual application form
